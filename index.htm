MindAttic Cares

MindAttic Cares is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to serving charitable, educational, and social missions that benefit the public. The organization provides digital access, support, and educational assistance. Its goal is to help individuals who lack the means to access education, entertainment, and resources in the digital space.

Child’s Play

This year, we are proud to focus our giving efforts on Child’s Play, a nonprofit that uses the power of gaming to improve the lives of children in hospitals.

Founded in 2003 by Mike Krahulik and Jerry Holkins of Penny Arcade, Child’s Play now supports 190+ hospitals and domestic violence shelters worldwide, reaching countless children. Their initiatives go far beyond providing games; they create meaningful therapeutic opportunities.

Games are more than entertainment. They provide comfort, distraction, empowerment, and joy during some of the hardest times in a child’s life. With your support, Child’s Play can continue to help children around the world Play Games, Feel Better™.

1. Overview & Goals

Purpose. Stage an immersive, late-90s charity party on New Year’s Eve that culminates in a theatrical midnight “Y2K power failure” — raising funds and in-kind support for Child’s Play.

Theme. What we were all doing on 12/31/1999 — N64 on CRTs, TRL-era music, Lisa Frank, slap bracelets, Surge and Mountain Dew, Totino’s Pizza Rolls, countdowns on TV. At 11:58 PM a pre-recorded “breaking news” bulletin warns of a grid collapse. At 12:00 AM the master breaker is pulled and the event ends in a controlled blackout.

Target. 250 GA + 30 VIP attendees (cap 320 incl. staff/sponsors), ~$50K gross / ~$10K net to Child’s Play after costs.

Success Criteria

  • Hit fundraising target (cash + in-kind) and finish in the black.
  • 95% uptime on consoles/arcades; zero safety incidents.
  • Blackout sequence executed flawlessly (emergency lights active, safe egress, positive guest sentiment).
  • Net Promoter Score ≥ 50 in post-event survey.
  • At least 3 returning sponsor commitments for next year.

2. How to Use This Playbook

This document is structured for an organizing committee of 4–8 leads. Read top-to-bottom once, then assign owners per section. The 12-Week Timeline is your spine; every other section feeds it. The Budget is your guard rail; recheck after every signed contract. The Master Checklists are your safety net for the final 72 hours.

Recommended Committee

Event Lead (final approver) · Producer (vendors, timeline) · Sponsorship Lead · Marketing Lead · Tech/AV Lead · Volunteer Coordinator · Finance/Compliance.

3. 12-Week Planning Timeline

Working backward from event day (ED). If your venue or municipal permits demand longer lead times, expand T−12 accordingly.

T−12 weeks — Foundation

  • Confirm 501(c)(3) status and fiscal sponsor letter ready for sponsors.
  • Set date, capacity target, ticket price tiers, and gross-revenue goal.
  • Open dedicated event bank sub-account; set up accounting tags.
  • Begin venue scouting (3–5 candidates); request walkthroughs.
  • Draft sponsor one-sheet and target list of 25–40 prospects.
  • Stand up event website (this page), reservation list, and email capture.

T−10 weeks — Lock the venue

  • Walkthroughs complete; verify circuits, dock, elevator, egress, ADA.
  • Sign venue license; pay deposit; secure date in writing.
  • File for required permits (special event, indoor amusement, food, liquor).
  • Request insurance quotes (GL 1M/2M + host liquor if applicable).
  • Place vendor holds: console partner, arcade row, furniture, AV/DJ.

T−8 weeks — Launch sales

  • Sponsorship outreach in earnest; 1:1 calls with top 10 prospects.
  • Open ticket sales (early-bird pricing for first 2 weeks).
  • Announce on socials with brand identity locked.
  • Begin volunteer recruitment (target: 18 volunteers, recruit 24).
  • Brief Child’s Play on the event; request quote & logo usage.

T−6 weeks — Production design

  • Finalize floor plan and power map; share with venue and electrician.
  • Confirm console counts (24 live + 4–6 spares) and arcade lineup.
  • Lock catering menu and bar plan; pay deposits.
  • Commission “Breaking News” bulletin video (3–4 week lead).
  • First sponsor commitments due; update budget.

T−4 weeks — Press & volunteers

  • Issue press release and pitch local media/podcasts.
  • Confirm all volunteers; send role packets and shift schedule.
  • Order signage, wristbands, sponsor wall, merch.
  • Finalize raffle & auction items; print bid sheets.
  • Lock paid social ad spend (peaks T−3 to T−1).

T−2 weeks — Tech rehearsal

  • Full cue-to-cue rehearsal of the midnight countdown + breaker pull (offsite or onsite).
  • Submit final headcount to caterer; confirm bar tab cap.
  • Reconfirm docks, security, elevator reservations, load-in window.
  • Print run-of-show, contact sheet, and emergency procedures.
  • Pre-pack load-in kit (gaff tape, ramps, tools, first aid, batteries).

T−1 week — Final confirms

  • Confirm every vendor with a phone call (not just email).
  • Distribute final volunteer schedule with radio channels.
  • Safety walkthrough with venue; sign blackout-procedure waiver.
  • Last marketing push: email blast, IG stories, “tickets running out.”

  • See Section 9 — Event-Day Run of Show.

4. Venue, Permits & Insurance

Target Spaces (~5,000–8,000 sq ft, ground-floor preferred, 200+ amp service):

  • Event lofts, gallery spaces, brewery taprooms, community halls, school gyms (after-hours), or convertible warehouse venues.
  • Built-in dock, freight elevator, and enough 20A circuits for ~80–100 amps of live load.
  • House A/V is a bonus; full BYOAV is acceptable.

Venue Action Steps

  • Licensing. Short-term license agreement; spell out hours, load-in, signage rights, alcohol policy, security minimums.
  • Walkthrough. Map circuits/panels, dock + elevator dimensions, egress routes, emergency-light controls, restroom counts.
  • Approvals. Get written sign-off on the intentional blackout sequence; coordinate with fire marshal if required.
  • Insurance. Certificate of Insurance naming owner/manager as additional insured; vendor COIs collected; host-liquor coverage if alcohol is served.
  • Permits. Special-event/indoor-amusement permit; temporary food permit; special-event liquor (if serving). Lead time varies by city — file early.
  • Life Safety. Confirm emergency lighting stays active during the breaker pull; entry signage informs guests; ADA compliance throughout.
Safety Non-Negotiable

The midnight blackout pulls only show-load circuits. Emergency egress lighting, exit signs, and fire systems must be on an independent, untouchable circuit. Get this in writing from the venue and the electrician of record.

6. Floor Plan & Power

Zones & Flow

  • 36″ minimum aisles; clear exit routes; food kept away from consoles; sound zoning so the “newsroom” remains audible at 11:58.
  • Cable ramps on every crossing; gaff-taped lines; rugs secured.

Suggested Layout

  • Front: Registration → Sponsor Wall → Merch/Donation kiosk.
  • Left: Arcade Row on dedicated circuits.
  • Center: Four Console Pods (each = 6 stations) with lounge seating.
  • Right: Tournament Feature Stage + audience seats.
  • Rear: Food/Drink counters + bussed high-tops.
  • Projection Walls: One continuous “MTV/TRL” loop; one “Newsroom” screen reserved for the 11:58 PM bulletin.

Power Worksheet (Pre-Build)

  • CRT + N64 station ≈ 1.5–2.0 A
  • Arcade cabinets avg 3–5 A; outliers up to 8 A
  • Audio/Projection ≈ 15–20 A
  • Food warmers/coolers ≈ 10–15 A
  • Target buffer: ≥20% per circuit

Circuit Planning

  • Console Pod (6 stations): ~12 A → place on two 20 A circuits.
  • Arcade Row (10 units): group by draw; no more than 3–4 per 20 A; isolate any 8 A machines.
  • AV & Lighting: dedicated 20 A.
  • Food: dedicated 20 A.
  • Label every circuit at the distro; complete a checklist before doors.

8. The Show: Countdown & Blackout

23:45 — DJ fades to low; house lights dim slightly.
23:50 — Host sets the stage; announces “news bulletin at 11:58 PM.”
23:58 — Pre-rendered “Breaking News” video rolls on all projectors (except emergency lights). Audio to full.
23:59:40 — Crowd countdown appears on-screen; host leads final 20 seconds.
00:00:00 — Stage Manager radios “GO”; Electrician throws the master breaker (pre-approved scope) excluding emergency egress.
+0:30 sec — Host on battery megaphone thanks attendees; staff activate lanterns; ushers guide exits.
+5:00 min — Emergency lights only; doors remain staffed; no re-entry.

Safety Controls

Emergency lighting on separate, protected circuit (tested same day). Battery megaphone + flashlights/lanterns staged at every usher post. Ushers pre-positioned at exits 15 minutes before midnight. Printed notice at entry: “Lights will go out at midnight as part of the show.” Anyone with mobility, vision, or sensory needs is briefed at check-in and assigned a buddy.

11. Sponsorship & Fundraising

Sponsorship Tiers

  • Millennium Sponsor — $5,000: Top-line logo on entrance wall, “news bulletin” credits, stage mentions, 6 VIP tickets, sponsor table.
  • Bug Fixer — $2,500: Station placards, digital/social promos, 4 VIP tickets.
  • Y2K Compliant — $1,000: Website & social mentions, entry signage, 2 VIP tickets.
  • In-Kind: Snacks, beverages, décor, printing, professional services. Placement based on retail value.

Sponsor Outreach Workflow

  1. Build target list of 25–40 prospects (alumni, local tech firms, retro/gaming brands).
  2. Send personalized email with one-sheet PDF; CC committee member with relationship.
  3. Follow up by phone within 5 business days.
  4. Confirmed sponsors get an onboarding packet: logo specs, deadlines, ticket allocation, photo release.
  5. Post-event recap sent within 10 days (see Section 17).

Fundraising Beyond Tickets

  • Silent Auction. 20–30 items, 90s-themed gift baskets, signed memorabilia, experience packages. Bid sheets at retro-styled tables; close at 22:00.
  • Raffle. Anchor prize (e.g., restored N64 in original box) at $10/ticket or $25 for 3. Drawing at 22:30.
  • Donation Kiosk. Tap-to-give terminal at entry; suggested amounts $20/$50/$100; show running total on screen during evening.
  • Live Ask. Two-minute moment at 21:30 from a Child’s Play hospital partner or family.
  • Corporate Match. Promote employer matching gifts on entry signage and email confirmations.

13. Compliance & Risk

Licensing & Permits

  • Public Place of Amusement / Indoor Special Event: engage local licensing agency for ticketed public entertainment.
  • Temporary Food: single-event food license if warming or serving. No home-prepared foods.
  • Alcohol (if any): Special-Event Retailer’s license or partnership with a licensed caterer; display license on-site.

Insurance

  • GL $1M / $2M aggregate; additional insureds: venue owner, building manager.
  • Host liquor coverage if applicable.
  • Vendor COIs collected and filed for every contracted vendor.

Life Safety

  • Pre-approved blackout plan with the building; electrician of record signs off in writing.
  • Emergency lights verified on an independent circuit or battery backup — tested same day.
  • ADA aisles 36″ minimum; exits marked and unobstructed at all times.
  • Trained ushers at every exit before midnight, with battery flashlights and lanterns.

Operations

  • Radio check every hour; comm hand-off at shift changes.
  • Incident log at the Safety desk; first-aid kit; water station for staff.
  • Crowd count at registration; hard hold if venue capacity reached.

Minors Policy

  • Under 16 with a guardian only; clear wristband color coding when alcohol is present.
  • Photography opt-out wristband available; staff briefed.

16. Show Assets & Scripts

Breaking News Video (2:00) — Script Beats

  • 0:00–0:20 Cold open: anchor desk; lower-third “Rolling Y2K Outages.”
  • 0:20–0:50 Vox pops of “IT admins” + b-roll of beige towers, BIOS screens.
